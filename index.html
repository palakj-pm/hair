Scoring guide
How each Yes / Mixed / No is defined - so any reviewer can verify by listening.
Consumer side - how the user felt
1. Were they reassured?
Yes Patient's tone shifted positively by the end. Signals: thanks / okay / will try, calmer responses, or explicit reassurance acknowledged.
Partial Some reassurance given by doctor but uncertainty remains - patient still hedging or asking the same Q.
Not really Patient still anxious / uncertain at end. Signals: same concern raised twice without resolution, no closure phrases.
2. Was their doubt resolved?
Yes Every concern the user raised was directly answered. No question left hanging.
Partial Most addressed, 1 or 2 left vague or punted to “app coach”.
No Key questions not answered; user repeated concerns; doctor moved on.
3. Engagement level?
High Long answers, volunteers info, asks follow-up questions on their own.
Medium Adequate but mostly reactive. Yes/no responses + occasional clarification.
Passive Monosyllabic. Doesn't volunteer anything. Doctor does 80%+ of the talking.
4. Confusion markers?
None No “I don't understand”, no repeated Qs, no long silences.
Few 1–2 confusion moments, partially resolved.
Many 3+ signals: repeated Qs, “samajh nahi aaya”, long silences after explanation.
5. Clear closure (next steps + what to expect)?
Yes All three covered: (a) clear next steps stated, (b) what to expect (timeline / shedding / when results show), (c) follow-up date or way to reach back.
Partial 2 of 3 covered (e.g. next steps + timeline but no follow-up scheduled).
No 0 or 1 of 3. Call ends without clear plan.
Doctor side - how the call was handled
6. Actively listening?
Yes Doctor references specific things the user said; follow-up Qs based on user's answers (e.g. “you said you used X for 2 years - why did you stop?”).
Mixed Mostly listening but some generic responses; doesn't probe deeper when user opens up.
Script-driven Follows checklist regardless of what user says. Skips past important reveals.
7. Personalised vs generic?
Personalised Recommendation tied to user's specific stage, history, and what they said. Names their case.
Mixed Some personalisation (mentions stage) but mostly standard regime.
Generic Standard script regardless of user's situation.
8. Completeness - covered all 5?
All 5 All five covered: (1) diagnosis (2) treatment (3) side effects (4) timeline (5) expectations.
3–4 of 5 Most but missing 1–2 (commonly: timeline or expectations skipped).
1–2 of 5 Only diagnosis + treatment; rest skipped.
9. Empathy shown?
Warm Doctor names user's anxiety, normalises (“many people have this at your age”), patient and warm tone.
Neutral Professional but neutral. No explicit emotional acknowledgment.
Clinical Clinical, rushed, or dismissive. User shows worry, doctor moves past it.
10. No product pushing?
Right products only Products match the diagnosed stage exactly. No upsell.
Borderline One borderline recommendation (e.g. adds a serum that's nice-to-have but not core).
Pushed extras Multiple products beyond what the stage requires. User showed cost concern; doctor ignored it.
Supporting tactics (layered onto the primary action)
Tactics aren't replacements for the primary action - they're moves the CRM agent layers in during the call. A user can have 0 to 3 tactics depending on signals seen.
💰 Discount offer / payment plan
Use when User shows cost concern (asked about billing, refund, replacement, or implicit cost via long silence after a heavy regime). Offer 3-month bundle, instalments, or % discount. Don't dilute the medical regime.
⭐ Social proof / "user like you"
Use when User has discontinuation history, anxiety/uncertainty about results, or fatigue after multiple cycles. Reference a peer-success story: "users who continued for 6 months saw growth", "users with similar Stage 3 and dandruff saw clear results", etc.
📉 Simplified regime
Use when Doctor stacked 5+ products and routine complexity is likely the blocker. Walk through habit-stacking or phased entry (e.g. dandruff treatment first, minoxidil after 15 days). Don't reduce the medical plan, just sequence it.
🩺 Senior doctor consult
Use when Results plateaued after multiple cycles, or there's a medical signal (e.g. genetic, hormonal, or non-AGA condition like alopecia areata) that needs specialist review. Escalate, don't recycle.
📊 Results check-in (no sales)
Use when User is in a medical pause, treatment fatigue, or has been silent so long that any sales push will erode trust. First touch is purely health-focused: "Did you see any change?" / "Did you go to the dermatologist?" Sales conversation comes later, only if relevant.
Confidence tag (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW)
HIGH - multiple corroborating phrases in transcript, or English transcript where keywords are clear.
MEDIUM - one mention or inferred from context. Should verify by listening to the audio.
LOW - ambiguous or extracted from a Hindi transcript where STT may have garbled product / brand names.
